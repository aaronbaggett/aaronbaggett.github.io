Hi, I'm Aaron Baggett and I am Instructor of Psychology at the University of Mary Hardin-Baylor in Belton, TX.

I currently teach undergraduate courses in research methods and statistics for the behavioral sciences.

My research revolves primarily around two broad objectives:

  1. To examine perceptual and cognitive skills involved in judgment and decision making among sport performers. Currently, I'm working to apply models estimating expertise in ball–strike judgments and decisions among Major League Baseball (MLB) umpires. See my projects page for more information on our methods and procedures.

  2. To assist in the development of statistical methodologies and computing processes for analyzing and visualizing MLB pitch-level data.
