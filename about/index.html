About This Site

This site has been a long-time-coming. I've dabbled in Wordpress a bit before and could never seem to find an effecient theme or web host. During my Ph.D. program at Baylor Univeristy I began experimenting with GitHub. I learned about GitHub Pages and quickly realized the potential for publishing flexible, creative, and user-designed content.

From there, it was on to locate the best framework. I tend to prefer minimal designs so I was initially drawn to Twitter Bootstrap. After having a little difficulty deploying a hybrid blog/static site with Bootstrap I came across JekyllBootsrap. I really like the content-focused delivery of Jekyll. I wanted the ability to write posts and author content in Markdown via RStudio, push it all to GitHub, all while avoiding so-called blogging software.

Finally, I came across Jonathan McGlone's site after browsing through a few examples powered by Jekyll. I really liked the minimal style and format–so much so that I decided to fork his repo and customize it to suit my needs. Thanks, Jonathan! In other words, since he licensed jmcglone.com under a Creative Commons Attribution 3.0 Unported License I was able to treat Jonathan's site as a template. If you're so inclined, feel free to do the same.
